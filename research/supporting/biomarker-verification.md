## 1. Function Health

**Verification Status:** [Multi-source verified]
**Sources:** functionhealth.com, brynelise.com deep-dive, mygenefood.com review, dannb.org review, Reddit r/Function_Health (multiple threads), TIME magazine article, organicallybecca.com

### Marketing vs Reality

| Metric | Marketing Claim | Verified Reality |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Biomarker count | "100+ lab tests" / "160+ lab tests" | ~110-120 directly measured individual markers across Annual + Mid-Year draws. The "160+" figure counts both draws combined + sub-components. |
| Draws/year | 2 included | VERIFIED: Annual Test (~110-120 markers) + Mid-Year Test (~60-70 markers) |
| Price | $365/year | VERIFIED (reduced from $499 in Nov 2025) |

### Key Discrepancies

1. **"160+ lab tests" inflation:** The 160+ number counts individual CBC sub-components, CMP sub-components, and both draws. Actual unique directly-measured biomarkers per draw cycle: ~110-120.
2. **B12 is NOT directly measured:** They test MMA (methylmalonic acid) as a functional proxy. While MMA is arguably a better indicator, marketing does not clarify this distinction.
3. **Omega-3 Index is NOT standard methodology:** They measure serum Omega-3 total and AA/EPA ratio, which differs from the standard OmegaQuant Omega-3 Index (RBC membrane EPA+DHA %).
4. **RBC Magnesium IS included:** Verified by brynelise.com deep-dive and member dashboard screenshots. This corrects earlier conflicting reports.
5. **Clinician notes marketed as "personalized review"** are widely reported as generic/AI-generated by multiple Reddit users and reviewers.

### Biomarkers Calculated but Marketed as Tested

- eGFR (calculated from creatinine)
- Non-HDL Cholesterol (TC minus HDL)
- Albumin/Globulin Ratio
- Iron Saturation % (Iron/TIBC)
- These are standard clinical calculations and are NOT misleadingly marketed by Function. They do not inflate their count with derived ratios the way Superpower does.

### Add-On Biomarkers Not in Base Count

- Folate (likely in Extended Vitamins add-on, price unknown)
- Selenium (likely in Extended Vitamins add-on, price unknown)
- Galleri multi-cancer ($899)
- Extended Autoimmunity ($249)
- Extended Thyroid ($399)
- Extended Heavy Metals ($49)
- MTHFR Gene ($129)

## 2. Superpower

**Verification Status:** [Multi-source verified]
**Sources:** superpower.com, Reddit r/biohackers, Function Health federal lawsuit filings (Jan 2026, Central District of California), Crown Counseling 8-month review, multiple comparison blogs, independent user biomarker-by-biomarker comparisons

### Marketing vs Reality

| Metric | Marketing Claim | Verified Reality |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Biomarker count | "100+ biomarkers" (Baseline) | ~55-65 directly measured. Remainder are ~35-40 calculated ratios and derived indices. |
| Advanced Panel | "+33 biomarkers" | ~20 additional directly measured + more ratios |
| Total with Advanced | "130+ biomarkers" | ~80-85 directly measured + ~40+ calculated |

### Key Discrepancies (MAJOR)

1. **CRITICAL: ~40% of "biomarkers" are calculated ratios.** Function Health filed a federal lawsuit (Jan 2026) specifically alleging this is misleading. Independent Reddit users confirmed: "When I counted, Function had 100+ biomarkers and Superpower's basic tier is closer to 60 distinct biomarkers. The rest seems to be made up with a lot of ratios."
2. **Baseline thyroid is substandard:** TSH, T3 Uptake, Total T4, Free T4 Index (T7) -- NOT Free T3 or Free T4 directly. These require the $179 Advanced Panel.
3. **Free Testosterone likely calculated** from Total T + SHBG, not directly measured via equilibrium dialysis.
4. **"24/7 clinical team" is weekday health coaches,** not physicians or clinicians.
5. **No Fasting Insulin in Baseline** -- requires Advanced Panel ($179 extra).
6. **Aggressive supplement upselling:** AI recommends $400+/month in supplements from their marketplace.
7. **Cancellation difficulties:** Multiple Trustpilot reports of no cancel button in dashboard; reports of FTC Click-to-Cancel regulation violations.

### Biomarkers Calculated but Marketed as Tested

- Free T4 Index (T7) -- calculated from T3 Uptake and Total T4
- eGFR
- Non-HDL Cholesterol
- Bioavailable Testosterone
- Free Androgen Index
- Iron Saturation %
- ~35 additional derived ratios (NLR, PLR, LMR, SII, SIRI, AIP, TyG, HOMA-IR, eAG, Atherogenic Coefficient, CRP/Albumin, etc.)

### Coverage of 45-Test Target Panel

| Status | Count | Details |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Base ($199) | ~20/45 | CBC, CMP, Lipid, HbA1c, GGT, Uric Acid, TSH (basic), Total T, Free T (calc), Estradiol, SHBG, DHEA-S, Vit D, Ferritin, Iron Panel, hs-CRP, ApoB, Cortisol |
| +Advanced ($388 total) | ~33/45 | Adds: Insulin, Free T3, Free T4, TPO, TgAb, LH, FSH, Prolactin, B12, Folate, Homocysteine, ESR, Lp(a), IGF-1, PSA |
| Missing from all tiers | 12/45 | C-Peptide, RBC Mg, Zinc, Selenium, Copper, Ceruloplasmin, Fibrinogen, NMR/LDL-P, LP-PLA2, OxLDL, Omega-3, Cystatin C |

## 12. Key Findings and Discrepancies

### Most Common Discrepancies Across Services

1. **Biomarker count inflation is universal.** Every service inflates to some degree by counting CBC sub-components, CMP sub-components, and calculated values. Superpower is by far the worst offender (~40% calculated ratios). Function Health and Hundred Health are the most honest -- their counts roughly match verified directly-measured markers.

2. **Calculated ratios marketed as "biomarkers":**
   - **Worst:** Superpower (~35-40 calculated ratios counted in "100+")
   - **Moderate:** SiPhox (~12 ratios in "59")
   - **Minimal:** Function Health, Hundred Health, Lifeforce (standard clinical calculations only)

3. **Thyroid antibodies (TPO Ab, TgAb) are frequently missing:**
   - Present in base: Function Health, SiPhox
   - Present in base: Parsley Health (TPO confirmed, TgAb unclear)
   - Missing from base: Hundred Health, Mito Health (both tiers), Lifeforce (all tiers)
   - Requires add-on: Superpower (Advanced Panel)

4. **Tests missing from ALL or nearly all services:**
   - C-Peptide (only SiPhox includes it)
   - RBC Magnesium (only Function Health, Parsley Health include it; most offer only serum)
   - Copper (only Parsley Health confirmed)
   - Ceruloplasmin (NO service includes it)
   - Fibrinogen (NO service includes it)
   - LP-PLA2 (NO service includes it)
   - Oxidized LDL (NO service includes it)
   - Omega-3 Index (proper RBC methodology -- NO service; Function has serum O3)

5. **Transparency varies wildly:**
   - **Most transparent:** Function Health (complete list published, multiple independent verifications), SiPhox (complete list on website)
   - **Moderate:** Superpower, Hundred Health, Lifeforce (lists partially available or verified through Ulta Lab Tests reference)
   - **Least transparent:** Wild Health, Fountain Life, Mito Health (lists NOT publicly available), Forward (misleading "86" count)

6. **"Clinician review" quality varies:**
   - **Best:** Wild Health (dedicated physician, but rushed), Lifeforce (board-certified longevity doctor + prescribing), Mito Health (1:1 consultation)
   - **Moderate:** Parsley Health (written notes + optional $200 consult), Hundred Health (100-day protocol)
   - **Worst:** Function Health (widely reported as AI-generated), Superpower ("weekday health coaches" not clinicians)

7. **Prescription capability is rare:**
   - **Can prescribe:** Lifeforce (hormones, peptides, medications), Wild Health (precision medicine physicians)
   - **Cannot prescribe:** Function Health, Superpower, Hundred Health, SiPhox, Mito Health, Parsley Health (Longevity Labs tier)

### Key External Findings

1. **Function Health filed a federal lawsuit against Superpower (Jan 2026)** in the Central District of California, alleging Superpower inflates biomarker counts with calculated ratios. This is independently corroborated by Reddit users who compared the two services biomarker-by-biomarker.

2. **SiPhox is the only service offering C-Peptide in its base panel** -- a test missing from every other service surveyed. C-Peptide distinguishes insulin resistance from beta-cell dysfunction and is on our 45-test target panel.

3. **Parsley Health appears significantly underrated** in the biomarker space. Despite marketing only "80+" biomarkers, their functional medicine approach includes RBC Magnesium, Copper, Zinc, Folate, B12, ESR, and Leptin -- markers that most larger-panel competitors (including Function Health and Hundred Health) miss. At $350/year, Parsley may offer the best 45-panel coverage per dollar if their full biomarker list confirms suspected inclusions.

4. **No single service covers the full 45-test target panel.** The universally missing tests (Ceruloplasmin, Fibrinogen, LP-PLA2, Oxidized LDL, Omega-3 Index) require gap-fill ordering through Marek Health, Jason Health, or individual lab tests.

5. **Forward is NOT a biomarker service** despite marketing "86 labs + tests." The included blood tests are basic primary care screenings; everything advanced requires insurance billing.

6. **Wild Health and Fountain Life are fundamentally different products** -- they are concierge/longevity medicine services where bloodwork is one component of a much larger (and much more expensive) offering. Direct biomarker-to-biomarker comparison is not meaningful.

7. **At-home collection (SiPhox, Lifeforce) vs lab draw (all others):** At-home convenience comes with tradeoffs. SiPhox's EasyDraw capillary collection showed Vitamin D discrepancies vs venous draws in independent testing. Lifeforce uses at-home venous phlebotomy which matches lab accuracy.

## Expert Consensus Synthesis

### What Most Experts and Reviewers Agree On:

1. **Function Health is the current market leader** for comprehensive biomarker testing. Nearly every review places it in the top 2-3. Its 100-160+ biomarkers, 2 draws/year, Quest Diagnostics network, and clinician review set the standard.

2. **$365/yr (Function) to $499/yr (Hundred) is the price range for comprehensive testing.** Ordering equivalent panels a la carte would cost $800-$1,500+, making membership services genuinely cost-effective for comprehensive panels.

3. **Biomarker count alone is insufficient for evaluation.** Key differentiators include: which specific markers are included (ApoB, Lp(a), NMR, hormones), interpretation quality, ongoing clinical support, and data portability.

4. **ApoB and Lp(a) are essential cardiovascular markers** -- both Attia and Huberman prioritize these. Services that include them in base membership (Function, Hundred) have an advantage over those requiring add-ons (Superpower).

5. **Wearable integration is the emerging differentiator** -- Hundred Health (Oura, Whoop, Apple Fitness), Whoop Advanced Labs, and Oura Health Panels lead here. Function Health notably lacks wearable integration.

6. **Biological age calculations are inconsistent across platforms** -- one reviewer got 37.3 (Function) vs 45.2 (Superpower) for the same person. Track trends within one platform, not across.

7. **2-4 draws per year is the recommended testing frequency** for active optimization, with quarterly being ideal.

8. **HSA/FSA eligibility is broadly available** across major providers.

### Areas of Disagreement:

1. **Value of comprehensive testing vs targeted testing.** Medical community (PCP perspective) warns of overtesting harms (anxiety, unnecessary follow-ups). Biohacking community views comprehensive data as empowering. No consensus.

2. **Clinician-led vs data-first approach.** Parsley Health argues clinician involvement is essential for safety. Function/Superpower argue democratized data access is the point. Depends on user sophistication.

3. **Rankings vary significantly by reviewer's priorities.** Outliyr ranks Hundred #1; Elo Health ranks Function #1; OptimizeBiomarkers gives best value to Ultrahuman. No universal winner.

4. **Business model sustainability.** Sherwood News and NPR report that blood testing is inherently unprofitable and may be a loss-leader for supplement/drug upsells. Long-term viability of subscription-only models is questioned.

5. **Hundred Health vs Function Health.** Too early to declare a winner -- Hundred launched December 2025 and has superior wearable integration but minimal track record. Function has 3+ years of operation and larger user base.

6. **Empirical Health's claims ($190 for 100+ biomarkers)** are significantly cheaper than competitors but only self-reported on their own marketing pages. Independent verification needed.

## Recommendations from Expert Review Research

1. **Function Health remains the safest choice** for comprehensive baseline testing given 3+ years of operation, largest biomarker panel at reasonable price ($365/yr), and broad expert endorsement. However, its lack of wearable integration and "closed system" data model are notable weaknesses for a Garmin user wanting CSV export.

2. **Hundred Health is the most promising newcomer** for users with wearables (Oura/Whoop/Apple -- NOT Garmin specifically), but its December 2025 launch means limited track record. Monitor closely.

3. **Superpower at $199/yr is the best budget entry point** and offers unique "Bring Your Own Data" feature, but base biomarker count is lower and key markers (Lp(a)) require paid add-ons.

4. **Combined-service strategy** (Function + Superpower at ~$565/yr for 3-4 draws) was recommended by the most experienced reviewer (healnourishgrow.com, 4 rounds of testing).

5. **Verify Empirical Health's $190 claims independently** before considering -- all comparison data is self-published marketing material.

6. **Be aware of the upsell model** -- base memberships may be loss-leaders designed to sell supplements, add-on tests, or medications. Budget for the base membership only and evaluate add-ons critically (Gene Food reviewer spent $1,366 on Function with add-ons vs $499 base).

7. **Garmin integration is not offered by any service reviewed.** Hundred Health supports Oura, Whoop, and Apple Fitness only. This is a gap for the reader profile.

8. **Data export/portability remains poorly documented** across most services. Function described as a "closed system." Superpower's "Bring Your Own Data" and InsideTracker's external data acceptance are the strongest data portability features identified.

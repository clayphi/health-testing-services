## Section A: Pricing & Membership

### A1. Price/Year
- **$199/year** ($17/month billed annually) for Baseline membership [Multi-source verified]
- **$399/year** for members in NY and NJ (90+ biomarkers only, no follow-up panel) [Multi-source verified]
- Price was reduced from $499 to $199 at some point post-launch (April 2025) [Single-source only — traksource.com]

### A2. Tier Options
Superpower does NOT have named tiers (e.g., "Silver/Gold"). Instead, it operates as a single base membership with add-on panels:
- **Baseline Membership** — $199/year (1 draw, 100+ marketed biomarkers)
- **+ Advanced Panel** — $189 add-on (bumps to 130+ marketed biomarkers)
- **+ Specialty Panels** — Cardiovascular ($159), Metabolic ($129), Autoimmunity & Celiac ($139), Nutrient & Antioxidant ($159), Methylation ($169), Environmental Toxins ($299), Heavy Metals ($129), Gut Microbiome ($239), Galleri Cancer ($849)
[Multi-source verified]

### A3. What Each Tier Includes
**Baseline ($199):**
- ONE comprehensive blood draw/year (~55-60 directly measured biomarkers + ~40 calculated ratios = "100+")
- AI-driven personalized action plan
- 17 health scores + biological age (BioAge) + Pace of Aging + Superpower Score
- Dashboard to track health data over time
- 24/7 concierge access via SMS (responses within 1 business day)
- 20% discount on marketplace supplements and prescriptions
- Wearable sync (Oura, WHOOP, Garmin — added in v1.0.6, March 2026)
- Upload past blood work
- Data download/delete capability

**Advanced Panel (+$189):**
- All baseline markers PLUS ~20 additional directly measured biomarkers
- Adds: Fasting Insulin, Free T3, TPO Antibodies, TgAb, FSH, LH, Prolactin, Progesterone, B12, Folate, Homocysteine, ESR, Lp(a), Lipoprotein Fractionation, ADMA, SDMA, IGF-1, PSA Total + Free, AMH
- Adds: 15-marker urinalysis
- Turnaround: 12-15 days (vs 5-7 days for baseline)
[Multi-source verified]

## Section B: Complete Biomarker Inventory

### CRITICAL CONTEXT: Measured vs Calculated Controversy

Function Health filed a federal lawsuit (January 26, 2026, Central District of California, Case 2:26-cv-00810) alleging Superpower's "100+ biomarkers" marketing is misleading. The lawsuit claims Superpower's baseline provides ~55 direct lab measurements, with the remainder being calculated ratios. A Reddit user independently confirmed: "closer to 60 distinct biomarkers. The rest seems to be made up with a lot of ratios." [Multi-source verified — lawsuit filings, ArentFox Schiff analysis, Reddit, Athletech News, SmartCompany]

**IMPORTANT NOTE ON PANEL CHANGES:** Third-party sites (bloodtestcomparison.com) list some Advanced-only markers (Free T3, Insulin, IGF-1, FSH, LH, etc.) as if they are in the baseline. Superpower's own marketing pages are deliberately vague. The authoritative source — superpower.com/biomarkers — clearly distinguishes baseline vs. advanced markers. The listing below follows the official /biomarkers page. [Single-source only for this assessment]

Additionally, Superpower's marketing pages sometimes reference "Free T3" and "Free T4" as included in their testing without clarifying these require the Advanced Panel (+$189). This blurring is consistent with the lawsuit allegations about misleading marketing. [Multi-source verified]

## Summary

**Superpower** is the cheapest entry point ($199/year) for membership-based comprehensive blood testing, but the headline "100+ biomarkers" count is misleading — approximately 55 are directly measured in the baseline, with the rest being calculated ratios that any service's raw data can produce. The Advanced Panel (+$189, total $388) adds critical markers (Fasting Insulin, Free T3, Lp(a), B12, Homocysteine, IGF-1, PSA) but still misses 11 tests from our 45-panel target.

**for a longevity-focused member:**
- the US is supported
- Garmin integration was added March 30, 2026 (quality unverified)
- Baseline alone ($199) covers only 20/45 target tests (44%) — insufficient
- Baseline + Advanced ($388) covers 33-34/45 (73-76%) — competitive but not leading
- 11 critical tests still require gap-fill via another service (est. $300+ additional)
- Total estimated cost for full 45-panel coverage: **$688-$858** (Superpower $388 + gap-fill)
- Supplement upselling ($400+/month) is the primary business model — the $199 membership is a loss leader
- Active federal lawsuit over biomarker count claims; doctored Reddit ad incident; cancellation difficulties
- iOS app is new (Jan 2026) and well-rated (4.9/5) but no Android app
- Data export exists but format unclear; no API access

**Verdict:** Good value for a basic health snapshot at $199. Competitive at $388 (with Advanced) for moderate coverage. But the misleading marketing, aggressive supplement upselling, cancellation difficulties, and active lawsuit are significant trust concerns. for a longevity-focused member wanting comprehensive baseline tracking with raw data access, the gap-fill requirements and trust issues make Superpower a weaker choice than services with higher directly-measured biomarker counts.

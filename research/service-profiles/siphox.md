## B. Complete Biomarker Inventory

### CRITICAL METHODOLOGY NOTE

SiPhox uses **at-home capillary blood collection** (fingerprick or EasyDraw upper arm patch), NOT venous blood draws. This fundamentally limits what can be tested:

- **No CBC (Complete Blood Count)** — capillary samples cannot reliably measure WBC, RBC, platelets, hemoglobin, hematocrit 
- **No CMP (Comprehensive Metabolic Panel)** — base panels omit BUN, creatinine, ALT, AST, ALP; Ultimate 360 adds some of these but accuracy from capillary samples is debated 
- **Small sample volume (~500 microliters via EasyDraw)** limits the number of assays possible 
- **Accuracy concerns:** SiPhox claims "95-99% correlation" with venous draws , but independent reviewers found **vitamin D discrepancies of 600%+ and testosterone discrepancies of 300%+** across different analyzer platforms . User reviews confirm significant discrepancies on some markers vs. Labcorp/Quest results. 

SiPhox itself states: **"SiPhox testing is not a substitute for your annual bloodwork."**

### What SiPhox Explicitly Cannot Test (Methodology Limitations)

These are impossible or unreliable with capillary/microsampling:
- **CBC** (requires venous blood for accurate cell counts)
- **NMR LipoProfile** (requires venous sample, specialized NMR equipment)
- **Heavy metals** (Lead, Mercury, Arsenic, Cadmium)
- **Omega-3/6 fatty acid index** (requires specific red blood cell membrane testing)
- **Urinalysis** (not a blood test)
- **Autoimmune panel** (ANA screen + pattern + titer — unclear if offered; one source suggests ANA Direct may be available but not confirmed in current panels)

## Summary

### DISQUALIFICATION ASSESSMENT

| Criterion | Status | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Provides numerical results | **PASS** | Yes — numerical values in dashboard and PDF |
| 50+ directly measured biomarkers | **BORDERLINE FAIL** | ~45 directly measured in Ultimate 360. Claimed "60" includes ~10-12 calculated ratios/derived indices. Depending on strict vs. lenient counting, this is at or just below the 50 threshold. |
| Available in the US | **PASS** | No TX restrictions found |

**RECOMMENDATION: BORDERLINE DISQUALIFICATION.** SiPhox Ultimate 360 claims ~60 biomarkers but only ~45 are directly measured. This falls short of the 50 directly-measured threshold. The base Longevity Essentials panel (~24 biomarkers) clearly fails.

### Key Strengths
- **Convenience:** Fully at-home, no lab visits, no needles (EasyDraw), 5-minute collection
- **Testing frequency flexibility:** Monthly to biannual — more frequent testing than any competitor
- **Unique markers:** C-Peptide (missing from most competitors), Cystatin C (in base Longevity Essentials)
- **Full thyroid panel:** TSH, Free T3, Free T4, TPOAb, TgAb — all in one test
- **Strong investor backing:** YC, Khosla, Intel Capital

### Key Weaknesses
- **NO CBC at all** — fundamental limitation of capillary blood methodology
- **No full CMP** — missing electrolytes (Na, K, Cl, CO2)
- **No fasting insulin** — critical metabolic marker absent
- **No NMR LipoProfile, no Omega-3, no heavy metals, no IGF-1** — major gaps vs. competitors
- **Accuracy concerns** are real and documented — at-home capillary collection introduces variability that venous draws avoid
- **Biomarker count is inflated** with calculated ratios (~10-12 of ~64 listed per standardized count)
- **~24/45 target panel coverage (53%)** — worst among compared services
- **Garmin integration uncertain** despite marketing claims
- **No medical leadership visible** — engineering company, not medical company
- **Sample failure risk** — user-collected samples can be insufficient/contaminated
- **Not a substitute for lab blood work** — SiPhox's own admission

### Cost Comparison Context
At $450/yr (Ultimate 360, biannual), SiPhox is comparable in price to Function Health ($365) or Hundred Health ($499) but delivers far fewer directly measured biomarkers and cannot match the breadth of a venous blood draw.

### Bottom Line
SiPhox Health is a **convenience-first, at-home testing service** that trades biomarker breadth and accuracy for ease of use and testing frequency. for a longevity-focused member, it cannot serve as a primary testing service. It may have value as a **supplementary monitoring tool** between comprehensive venous draws, but the accuracy concerns, limited panel, and lack of CBC/CMP make it unsuitable as a standalone service for serious health optimization.

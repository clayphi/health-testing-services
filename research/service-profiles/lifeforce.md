## Summary

**Lifeforce** is a clinician-led longevity medicine platform that differentiates itself from pure testing services (Function Health, Superpower) by offering **prescribing capability** (testosterone, peptides, hormones) and **quarterly clinician consultations**. It is fundamentally a **telehealth + testing + pharmacy** hybrid rather than a pure biomarker testing service.

**Strengths for longevity-focused profile:**
- Prescribing capability for hormone optimization (TRT, peptides, clomiphene)
- At-home blood draws (convenience)
- Quarterly monitoring on monthly plan (4 draws/year)
- Lifescore with biological age component
- HSA/FSA eligible
- Available in the US

**Weaknesses for longevity-focused profile:**
- **Expensive:** $1,838/yr (monthly) or $599/yr (annual, but only 2 draws + 1 consult)
- **Limited biomarker breadth:** ~26 clinically distinct analytes in base panel; key longevity markers (Cystatin C, B12, Folate, Omega-3, Free T3, iron panel, autoimmune) missing
- **No wearable integration** (Garmin sync not available)
- **No confirmed data export** (PDF/CSV) -- problematic for raw data access goal
- **Only 10 states** (CA, CO, AZ, TX, FL, WA, NY, GA, IL, OH) -- the US is included, but users outside these states cannot access the service, unlike Function Health (48 states) or Wild Health (50 states via telehealth)
- **Supplement upsell model** -- conflict of interest concerns
- **Add-on panels expensive:** $200 each to fill gaps, and even with all 4 ($800), still missing many target biomarkers

**Best suited for:** Someone specifically seeking hormone optimization / TRT with monitoring, who wants clinician access bundled with testing. Less suited for someone primarily wanting comprehensive biomarker breadth and raw data access.

**Competitive position:** More expensive with fewer biomarkers than Function Health ($499/yr, 100+ markers) or Hundred Health ($499/yr, 117 markers), but uniquely offers prescription therapeutics and clinical consultations. Competes more directly with telehealth TRT clinics than with pure testing services.

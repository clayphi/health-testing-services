## Key Findings for longevity-focused profile

1. **InsideTracker** is the strongest fit for a longevity-focused member among newly discovered services — it is the ONLY one with native Garmin integration, has 15+ years of track record, allows uploading 30 past result sets per year, and offers biological age calculation. However, it is expensive for what you get ($829/yr for ~48 biomarkers, 2 draws) and lacks several longevity-critical markers (no Lp(a), no Homocysteine, limited thyroid).

2. **WHOOP Advanced Labs** offers strong biomarker breadth (65 markers including Lp(a), Homocysteine, Omega-3 Index) at a competitive price, but requires a WHOOP strap (no Garmin support) and has no physician access.

3. **Ultrahuman Blood Vision** has the broadest category coverage (autoimmune, heavy metals, urinalysis) at a competitive $499/yr, but individual biomarker names are not publicly verified, Garmin integration is indirect, and the company is newer with mixed Trustpilot reviews.

4. **Geviti** is the only qualifying service with prescribing ability (TRT, HRT, GLP-1s, peptides) and includes physician visits, but at $1,529/yr it is the most expensive option and lacks confirmed Garmin integration or data export.

5. **Empirical Health** is the best value ($399 for 3 draws) but is heart-health-focused with notable gaps (no hormones, limited thyroid, no advanced longevity markers).

6. **OneTwenty** has the most ambitious vision (quarterly draws, Garmin support, 500+ wearable signals) but is in closed beta with ~25 users — not a viable option today.
